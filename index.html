2 The Property
?Purchase PriceThe contracted price you'll pay to acquire the property. Everything flows from this number.Purchase Price
?After Repair Value (ARV)What the property will be worth fully renovated. Your ceiling on a flip. Be conservative — an optimistic ARV is how investors lose money.ARV
?Rehab CostEstimated total renovation cost. Always add a contingency — rehabs almost always run over.Rehab Cost
?Rehab ContingencyExtra % for surprises. 10–15% is standard. Use 20% for older homes or uncertain scopes. This money gets spent.Contingency
?Annual Property TaxLook this up — don't estimate. Property taxes vary wildly by market and county and directly affect every strategy's cash flow.Annual Property Tax
?Annual InsuranceAnnual landlord/investment property policy. Typically $800–2,000/yr. Not the same as homeowner's insurance — make sure you have the right policy.Annual Insurance
?Annual HOAHomeowners association fees. Enter 0 if none. Non-negotiable — model at full cost. Also verify HOA rules before buying for STR.Annual HOA
?Monthly Utilities (Flip/STR)Estimated monthly utilities while holding. Used for flip holding cost calculation and STR operating costs. LTR tenants typically pay their own utilities.Monthly Utilities

5 Rental Details
?Monthly RentMarket rent after rehab. Be conservative — overestimating rent is a top investor mistake.Monthly Rent
?Vacancy Rate% of year unit sits empty. 8% ≈ 1 month/year. Use 10–12% first year or softer markets.Vacancy
?Property Management% of collected rent. Typically 8–10%. Model it even if self-managing — you won't always.Prop Mgmt
?CapEx Reserve% for big-ticket repairs: roof, HVAC, water heater. 8–12% standard. Don't skip this.CapEx
?Maintenance Reserve% for ongoing repairs. 5–8% common post-rehab.Maintenance
?Annual AppreciationExpected annual value increase %. National avg 3–4%. Long-term projections only — don't count on appreciation to make a deal work.Appreciation
?Annual Rent GrowthExpected annual rent increase %. National avg ~2–3%. Used in projections.Rent Growth

5 Short-Term Rental Details
?Avg Nightly RateBlended average across peak and off-peak. Don't use your best-case rate as the average.Avg Nightly Rate
?Occupancy Rate% of nights booked annually. 60–65% is realistic. 75%+ is a top performer. Don't use best-case.Occupancy Rate
?STR ManagementSTR managers charge 20–30% of gross revenue vs 8–10% for LTR. High because of guest communication and turnover coordination.STR Mgmt
?Platform FeeAirbnb charges ~3% host fee. VRBO varies. Deducted from gross revenue before management fee.Platform Fee
?Cleaning Fee / StayCost per guest turnover. At 62% occupancy on avg 3-night stays, you're turning over ~75 times/year. Adds up fast.Cleaning/Stay
?Avg Stay LengthAverage nights per booking. Used to calculate cleaning frequency. Typical STR is 2–4 nights.Avg Stay Length
?Furnishing CostOne-time cost to fully furnish: furniture, linens, kitchen, decor. Typically $15–30K for a SFH. Added to cash required.Furnishing Cost
?Monthly UtilitiesSTR landlord pays all utilities. Budget $200–500/mo. Higher than LTR because guests use more.Monthly Utilities
?Monthly SuppliesConsumables restocked monthly: toiletries, paper goods, coffee, etc. $50–150/mo typical.Monthly Supplies
?STR InsuranceAnnual short-term rental policy. More expensive than landlord insurance — budget $1,500–3,000/yr.STR Insurance
?Annual License / PermitMany cities require STR permits and annual fees. Research your market — some cities ban STR entirely.License/Permit
?CapEx Reserve% of revenue for big-ticket repairs. Use 6–10% — STR guests cause more wear and tear than LTR tenants.CapEx Reserve
?Annual AppreciationExpected annual value increase %. National avg 3–4%.Appreciation
ℹ️ Property tax (—), insurance uses STR policy above, HOA (—) pulled from Step 1.
